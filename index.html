Paula Bennett made a comment regarding with the current PM

  • Rosie Nelson
  • February 20, 2020
  • Uncategorized
  • 0 Comment

Paula Bennett is a politician from New Zealand who serves as National Party Deputy Leader and MP for Upper Harbor. She worked as New Zealand’s deputy prime minister from December 2016 to October 2017.

Bennett formerly represented the Waitakere electorate, which had been removed before the general election in 2014. Until October 2017, she retained the Cabinet portfolios of State Services, Women, Tourism, Police, and Climate Change Issues before a Labor / NZ First coalition replaced the National Party in government.

Bennet is currently linked with some problem when she said the Prime Minister is “running scared,” blaming her for failing to take action against the NZ First leader. 

Her remarks come as the Serious Fraud Office investigates donations made to the New Zealand First Foundation and claims that Mr. Peters had filmed and accompanied journalists on their investigation report.

Yet just recently, Ms. Bennett has told media that questions should be asked by the Prime Minister.

“Why did he say we took the photos and then retracted 24 hours later. I think she’s running scared that he may pull the plug and I don’t think they’re ready for an early election,” she says.

By not asking any questions, Ms. Bennett says she believes that Ms. Ardern “supports this behavior.”
